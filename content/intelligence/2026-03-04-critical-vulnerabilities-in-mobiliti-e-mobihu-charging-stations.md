The vulnerabilities in Mobiliti's e-mobi.hu charging stations, with a CVSS v3 score of 9.4, pose significant risks to critical infrastructure. Attackers could exploit these flaws to gain administrative access or disrupt services entirely through DoS attacks. The missing authentication for critical functions and improper restriction of excessive authentication attempts highlight poor security practices in the implementation. This directly impacts electric vehicle charging stations, which are integral to modern transportation infrastructure. Operators must prioritize patches and network monitoring to mitigate these risks. The broader implication is a potential threat to the reliability of smart city systems, underscoring the need for stricter security standards in IoT and industrial control systems.

# Weekly Threat Intelligence Digest: 2025-W08 (February 17 - February 23)

## Executive Summary
This week highlighted two significant incidents in the cybersecurity landscape. The Ghost ransomware group targeted critical infrastructure globally, exploiting known vulnerabilities. Additionally, a record-breaking $1.5 billion theft occurred at Bybit, attributed to North Korean hackers. Notably, no new CVEs were tracked, underscoring attackers' reliance on known vulnerabilities.

## Critical & High Priority

### **Ghost Ransomware Targeting Critical Infrastructure**
- **What Happened**: The Ghost (Cring) ransomware group, linked to China, exploited known vulnerabilities in internet-facing services across 70 countries.
- **Who's Affected**: Targets include energy, healthcare, and financial sectors. Exploited CVEs: CVE-2018-13379, CVE-2021-34473, CVE-2021-34523, CVE-2021-31207.
- **Advisory**: CISA, FBI, and MS-ISAC urge patching known vulnerabilities and adopting defensive measures.

### **Bybit Cryptocurrency Exchange Theft**
- **What Happened**: $1.5 billion in Ethereum stolen from Bybit's cold wallet, the largest crypto theft to date.
- **Who’s Behind It**: North Korean state-sponsored actors likely involved.
- **Impact**: Highlights vulnerabilities in crypto exchanges' security protocols.

## Notable Developments
This week lacked medium or low severity incidents. However, ongoing trends like supply chain attacks and APTs targeting financial sectors remain critical areas for monitoring.

## Vulnerability Landscape
No new CVEs were tracked this week. This suggests attackers are focusing on known vulnerabilities, emphasizing the need for proactive patching and monitoring of existing issues.

## Recommended Actions
- **Patch Known Vulnerabilities**: Prioritize CVEs linked to Ghost ransomware.
- **Crypto Exchange Security**: Enforce MFA, regular audits, and threat detection.
- **Incident Response Drills**: Strengthen readiness with NIST-aligned frameworks.
- **Employee Training**: Combat phishing and social engineering.

## Looking Ahead
Next week, expect continued focus on critical infrastructure attacks and crypto sector threats. Monitor for emerging ransomware trends and state-sponsored campaigns targeting financial institutions.

Stay vigilant and proactive in addressing known vulnerabilities to mitigate evolving cyber risks.
